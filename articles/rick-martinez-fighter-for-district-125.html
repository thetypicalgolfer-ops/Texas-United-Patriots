Candidate Spotlight

Rick Martinez: The Servant-Leader San Antonio — and Texas — Has Been Waiting For

A soldier's son. A self-made man. A neighbor who shows up when the lights go out. A generational leader stepping into the arena for Texas House District 125 — and carrying a future the whole Lone Star State can feel coming.

April 17, 2026 | 9 min read | Texas United Patriots Staff
Spread the Word

In an age of slick-talking politicians and hollow photo ops, San Antonio has produced something increasingly rare: a candidate whose résumé was written by his actions, not his press releases. Ricardo "Rick" Martinez — son of a U.S. Army soldier, entrepreneur, philanthropist, and neighbor — is running for the Texas House of Representatives in District 125 not because he's chasing a title, but because he refuses to watch his community be failed by people who never knew it.

This is a man who built something from nothing. This is a man who feeds the hungry on weekends when no newspaper is watching. This is a man who believes, without apology, that the dignity of the human person is a gift from God — and that government exists to protect it, not to ration it. In a state that already leads the nation in courage, Rick Martinez is the next voice Texas needs in the fight.

And make no mistake: the Texans watching this race closely are not only watching District 125. They are watching a leader whose character, clarity, and courage are exactly the qualities the Lone Star State will need in its highest offices for the next generation. But first things first — and Rick is the first one to say so. The road to a freer, stronger Texas starts with one seat, one district, one race. This race.

Forged on a Military Base. Anchored in Faith.

Rick Martinez was born on a U.S. Army installation in Germany, the son of a soldier. He will tell you, without pride but without hesitation, that he learned three things in that household before he learned anything else: discipline, service, and love of country. Those are not slogans he picked up from a consultant. They are the air he breathed before he could speak.

From military barracks to a high school in Corpus Christi, from college in Lubbock — close to his sister, because family was never optional — to the streets of San Antonio, where he has made his home for more than a decade, Rick has carried those values with him like a compass. Ask anyone who knows him: he does not leave home without them.

"My father taught me that serving others is not a hobby. It's the reason you exist. Every morning I wake up and ask the Lord: put me where I'm useful."

— Rick Martinez

A Builder, Not a Bureaucrat

Rick Martinez is not a career politician. He has never drawn a government paycheck. He has never lobbied an agency. What he has done is build a company — from a single idea at a kitchen table into a real, tax-paying, job-creating Texas business that serves customers and honors its commitments.

That matters. It matters because the Texas Capitol is already overflowing with men and women who have never met a payroll, never fixed a broken truck, never sweated over a quarterly tax bill. Rick has done all three. When he speaks about property taxes, he is not repeating a talking point. He is describing what it feels like to write the check.

And when Rick talks about the dignity of work, he means it in a way that polished politicians never can. He has been the small-business owner who skipped his own paycheck so his employees could take theirs home. That is leadership. That is the kind of man you want between your family and a runaway government.

The Neighbor Who Shows Up

You can tell the measure of a man by what he does when nobody is watching. Rick Martinez founded his own charity. He volunteers with the Loving Heart Foundation, the San Antonio Food Bank, and the Ferrari Kid Foundation. He carries meals to homeless Texans and to veterans who gave everything for this country and came home to a government that forgot them.

When the floods tore through South Texas, Rick was not on a podium calling for "thoughts and prayers." He was on the ground, phone in hand, rallying local businesses to donate supplies, water, and hot meals to families who had lost everything. When first responders came off a brutal shift, Rick was there with food and with the thing they needed most — a community that said, out loud and in public, thank you.

This is not the charity of a man angling for office. This is the life of a man who was already living it, years before a ballot was ever printed. Scripture tells us that faith without works is dead. Rick Martinez's works have been alive in San Antonio for over a decade.

"I don't need a title to serve my neighbors. I already serve them. I'm running so San Antonio finally has someone in Austin who will keep serving them."

— Rick Martinez, announcing his candidacy

The Fight for District 125

Texas House District 125 is not a safe seat for the soul of South Texas. It is a frontline. Working families in this district are being squeezed between skyrocketing property taxes, a border crisis created by Washington, an energy grid stretched thin by lawless federal mandates, and a healthcare system that treats patients like case numbers.

Rick Martinez has walked into that fight with a clear, concrete platform — not vague promises, but battle plans:

shield

Back the Blue

Fully support law enforcement. Hold violent criminals accountable. End the "catch and release" culture that treats officers like the problem and criminals like the victim.

home

Kill the Homestead Tax

Expand the homestead exemption. Compress school tax rates. Work toward the ultimate goal: eliminating the homestead property tax so no Texan is ever renting their own home from the government.

volunteer_activism

Affordable Healthcare

Break the bureaucratic stranglehold on medicine. Empower doctors and patients. Deliver accessible, affordable healthcare without handing Texas over to federal one-size-fits-all mandates.

bolt

Energy & Opportunity

A modern, dependable energy grid. Real infrastructure investment. Good jobs built on Texas oil, gas, and ingenuity — not on Washington's green fantasies.

No More RINOs. No More Promises Without Delivery.

Let's be honest about something too many Texans are finally saying out loud: for too long, our state has been led by "Republicans In Name Only" — RINOs who campaign like conservatives and govern like consultants. They raise money on faith, family, and freedom, then spend it negotiating with the very bureaucracies that are crushing us. They promise to end the property tax, year after year — and year after year your bill still goes up. They promise to secure the border — and the cartels still operate like they own South Texas. They promise to stand against DEI and gender ideology — then quietly let taxpayer dollars fund both under new acronyms.

The current leadership in Austin has had every advantage a governor could ask for — a conservative Legislature, a booming economy, a mandate from the voters — and has still managed to leave Texans waiting. Waiting for real property tax abolition. Waiting for a border truly sealed. Waiting for an energy grid that doesn't fail in a cold snap. Waiting for universities that actually answer to the taxpayers who fund them. Texans are done waiting. And the establishment that keeps asking for patience is running out of the one thing it cannot print: credibility.

Rick Martinez is not cut from that cloth. He does not owe a single lobbyist, a single consultant, or a single donor class a single favor. He has never needed a title to do the right thing — the food he has delivered, the families he has helped, the first responders he has fed all prove that. In Austin, he will not "grow" into the establishment. The establishment will feel the floor shift when he walks in.

"The people of Texas didn't vote for a brand. They voted for promises. It's time the people we send to Austin actually keep them."

— Rick Martinez

A Bigger Horizon — But First, the Work in Front of Him

It is hard to sit with Rick Martinez for five minutes without realizing you are talking to a man with the temperament, the clarity, and the moral spine that this state will eventually need in its highest office. Seasoned political observers in San Antonio have begun quietly using the phrase "the next generation of Texas leadership" when his name comes up — and nobody who has watched him in a room full of families, first responders, or small-business owners disagrees.

But Rick Martinez is not running for Governor. Not today. Not yet. And he will be the first to tell you that is by design. Men who skip steps do damage. Men who want the spotlight more than the work do more damage still. Rick is running for the Texas House because that is where this fight is being fought right now — in classrooms, in sheriff's offices, in property tax appraisal rolls, in the kitchens of families who are one unexpected bill away from losing their home.

Win District 125, and San Antonio gets the representative it has been praying for. Win District 125, and Texas gets something it has not had in a very long time: a principled conservative leader whose résumé is not written in press releases but in the lives of the people he has already served. The road that follows — whatever road the Lord lays in front of him — begins with this vote, this donation, and this neighbor you tell tomorrow morning.

"I'm not here to climb a ladder. I'm here to serve San Antonio. If God wills more later, He will say so. Right now, my job is District 125."

— Rick Martinez

Why This Race. Why Now.

Make no mistake: the people who have spent decades mismanaging South Texas families do not want Rick Martinez to win. They have already begun the smears. They will call him extreme. They will call his faith "dangerous." They will call his love of country "divisive." This is the oldest playbook in politics — and it is running out of pages.

San Antonio is not fooled. San Antonio families know the difference between a photo-op and a food drop. They know the difference between a press release and a paycheck. They know the difference between a politician who lectures them about their values and a neighbor who lives those values on their street.

Rick Martinez is that neighbor. And come Election Day, District 125 has a choice: more of the same bureaucratic indifference, or a proven fighter with calloused hands, a clear conscience, and a spine made of Texas steel.

"San Antonio families deserve a voice that answers to God and to them — nobody else. No lobbyists. No bosses in Austin. Just the truth, and a fight."

— Rick Martinez
